SCENARIO 2

STAR RIVALS

Space Arms Race

  • • By 2040, space has become a central arena of strategic competition among major powers. Governments invest heavily in advanced launch systems, satellite networks, orbital surveillance, and counter-space capabilities, treating space as a critical domain for national security. Military and civilian technologies are increasingly intertwined, with dual-use infrastructure supporting both commercial services and defence objectives. Although technological innovation continues and launch costs decline, progress is driven primarily by geopolitical rivalry rather than international collaboration.

Fragmented Markets

  • • At the same time, the global space economy is divided into competing blocs with separate standards, regulatory frameworks, and industrial ecosystems. Rival satellite constellations and incompatible technical systems limit interoperability and cross-border cooperation. Companies must align with specific political spheres to access funding, supply chains, and market opportunities. While commercial activity remains strong, global integration weakens, and the industry operates within parallel, politically shaped markets rather than a unified space economy.
